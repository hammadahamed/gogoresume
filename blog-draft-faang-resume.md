# How to Tailor Your Resume for FAANG Jobs (Using AI)

Getting into FAANG (Facebook, Amazon, Apple, Netflix, Google) is tough. Recruiters receive thousands of applications daily. If your resume doesn't immediately signal "match," it's rejected by the ATS (Applicant Tracking System) before a human even sees it.

Here's how to beat the system and tailor your resume perfectly for top-tier tech roles.

## Why Generic Resumes Fail

Big tech companies use sophisticated ATS software to filter candidates. These systems look for specific keywords from the job description (JD). A generic resume might list your skills, but if it doesn't use the *exact* terminology from the JD, you might be scored low.

## Step 1: Analyze the Job Description

Don't just skim. Read the JD line by line. Look for:
- **Hard Skills:** specific languages (Python, Java), frameworks (React, TensorFlow), or tools (Kubernetes, AWS).
- **Soft Skills:** "leadership," "cross-functional collaboration," "mentorship."
- **Action Verbs:** "architected," "deployed," "scaled," "optimized."

## Step 3: Quantify Your Impact

FAANG recruiters love data. Instead of saying "Improved performance," say:
> "Optimized API latency by 40%, resulting in a 15% increase in user retention."

Use the **XYZ formula** (Accomplished [X] as measured by [Y], by doing [Z]).

## Step 4: Optimize for ATS

- **Keep it simple:** Use standard headings (Experience, Education, Skills).
- **Avoid graphics:** Tables and columns can confuse older ATS parsers.
- **Use a standard font:** Arial, Helvetica, or Calibri.
